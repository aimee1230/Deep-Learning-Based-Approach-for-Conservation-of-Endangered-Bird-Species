# A Scalable AI Approach to Bird Species Identification for Conservation and Ecological Planning

This repository contains the implementation of our research paper, **"A Scalable AI Approach to Bird Species Identification for Conservation and Ecological Planning,"** published in **IEEE Access**.

📄 **Paper:** https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2025.3607597

---

## Overview

Bird species identification plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation, ecological monitoring, and biodiversity research. This project presents a deep learning-based bird classification system that leverages **transfer learning** with **ResNet-50** to accurately identify bird species from images.

The proposed approach was trained on the **100 Bird Species** dataset, containing **85,000 images** across **525 bird species**, including endangered and critically endangered birds.

---

## Key Features

- Transfer learning using **ResNet-50**
- Classification of **525 bird species**
- Trained on **85,000 bird images**
- Achieved **87% classification accuracy**
- Successfully identified endangered and critically endangered bird species
- Supports wildlife conservation and ecological planning

---

## Dataset

- **Dataset:** 100 Bird Species Dataset
- **Number of Images:** 85,000+
- **Number of Classes:** 525

> **Note:** The dataset is not included in this repository due to licensing restrictions.

---

## Model

- Backbone: ResNet-50
- Framework: TensorFlow / Keras
- Task: Multi-class Image Classification
- Learning Strategy: Transfer Learning

---

## Results

| Metric | Value |
|---------|------:|
| Bird Species | 525 |
| Images | 85,000+ |

The model demonstrated strong performance in identifying endangered and critically endangered bird species, highlighting its potential for real-world conservation applications.
